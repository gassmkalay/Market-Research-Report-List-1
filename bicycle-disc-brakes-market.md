<p><p>The bicycle disc brakes market is primarily divided into two types: mechanical and hydraulic disc brakes. Mechanical disc brakes use a cable system to activate the brake calipers, offering easier maintenance and affordability, making them popular among casual riders. In contrast, hydraulic disc brakes utilize fluid pressure for a more powerful and responsive braking experience, appealing to performance-oriented cyclists seeking superior modulation and efficiency. Both types contribute to enhanced safety and performance in various cycling conditions.</p></p>
